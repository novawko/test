Kanpolos

Scientific Classification:

  • Kingdom: Animalia
  • Phylum: Chordata
  • Class: Mammalia
  • Infraclass: Allotheria
  • Order: Chordonti
  • Family: Kanpidae
  • Genus: Kanpo
  • Species: Kanpolo
  • Binomial Name: Kanpo squinini
Kanpolos are a semi-aquatic species native to Jovalan, they have 2 subspecies, Freshwater Kanpolos and Deep Sea Kanpolos. They all live in the ocean or around water and love to make their homes out of brightly coloured things. Like coral, which is the main thing they build their homes out of. They live in colonies and are normally lead by one Kanpolo who starts the colony from scratch on their own until other Kanpolos move in.

Lifestyle & Behavior

Kanpolos live underwater in the sea usually on the sea floor. They can breath underwater and land no problem, though they can die if you cover the parts where they breath from. They make their homes out of Coral and usually pick the most colourful ones. They are usually Diurnal but it’s up to you what times of day they are up. They also taste like fish but pretty rich, sort of like prawn. They are hunted by other species, they have natural predators. Which are the ginormous land sharks, called the Bood Shark. They like to camouflage under the sand and wait for the Kanpolo to be close enough, usually during the time when Kanpolo sunbathe. Before bursting out of the sand and snapping them up whole. You can tell that they totally fear the Bood Shark, imagine being a human on Jovalan, you'd basically be an ant compared to it. They are also hunted by alien species called the Cracke-D which are a cephalopod-like species that has a appetite for their rich meat.

Kanpolos are more vulnerable on land, due to being a bit more slower on land than on water, due to the hunting and predators. They are naturally anxious on land, so if you approach them they will mostly scream/screech and go on the defensive almost like a cat, but would run away if it had the chance. They also can speak, but they usually talk once they’ve warmed up to you. In the sea they usually mimic sounds, like whale calls. They usually do whale calls/noises, they also make a bubbly purr sometimes, usually when happy or just whenever they feel like it.

Kanpolos also can be a bit (or more) defensive of their (lover) partner, usually the male Kanpolo’s are but females can be too.

Gestation Period

Starting with the gestation period of a Kanpolo: They start in the womb like a mammal their gestation period is about 14 months or more. The womb sac is inside the mother Kanpolos pelvis area, and expands out from the pelvis area as the gestation period goes, usually bigger if the Kanpolo is carrying more than 3. Because the Kanpolo can carry about 5 babies in their womb.

The embryo start as the size of a kidney bean (6mm), in a small fertilised fish like egg, the egg does not have a outer shell it’s almost like a slimy ball. By 10 weeks the embryo is a foetus (Month 3) and it is very visible inside the egg, the egg would be the size of a medium average chicken egg. (40.61mm). By 20 weeks (Month 5) the mother Kanpolo is in their First Trimester, the foetus being the size of a goose egg at this time. (3 inches). By (Month 9) the mother is in their Second Trimester, the foetus is the size of a average human baby.

(19 inches), they lay an egg, which the foetus is inside. When the egg comes out it has no outer shell and has a sac around the outside of it, of course it’s see through and fragile. But to protect it the sac helps the egg float down onto the sea floor so when it touches the sea floor it won’t be damaged. The sac around it protects it by releasing out fluid slowly. The outside of the egg which was protected by the sac after a while becomes a outer shell, which is hard to protect the foetus inside. By the 14th month the egg hatches and a little baby Kanpolo cub climbs out and is the size of a elephant calf (1 metre). Sometimes they are born sometime after the 14th month, but if they are born before the 14th month it’s usually something to be concerned about. But they are usually fine. When they are fully grown, they are the height of a fully grown elephant, or taller depending on the Kanpolo.

Beliefs

Kanpolos have a religion called Kutumuk, where they believe when they pass away, they become a eel creature based on a real eel species found on their planet. That has crystallised coral on its back, it's basically a god like creature, though they haven't been proven to exist, the real eels themselves look a bit different than their religion depicts. They call the crystallised eel, Kiutu(s).

Kanpolos when they see their kind ill, they'd think 'Oh, this is probably some common cold and they'll get over it'. 'No problem'. Kanpolos haven't learnt about germ theory or have scientifically proven medical treatment methods. As they do not have science or scientists, as they haven't exactly 'discovered' it. They usually let their wounds treat themselves or attempt to experiment with plants or herbs found around the ocean, like seaweed for example. They believe seaweed can treat infected wounds.

Kanpolos do not have technology and would be considered 'behind' compared to humans. They mainly use rocks as tools and build their homes out of corals, live in burrows they create and keep held up using coral as pillars or sandstone, etc. They can be described as 'tribal' or 'primitive', (not in a discriminatory way of course), they are very smart, it just takes time to learn new things. They call bullets 'arrows' and if you showed them a X'ray they'd most likely be scared of it as they are scared of new things and take some convincing or time to warm up to it.

Kanpolos prefer to go by their names instead of using pronouns, for example they rarely ever use She/He/Them. As gender isn't really something they care about usually, as they believe their names make them, them, not their gender. Kanpolos do not know of/have any other gender terms beside Female/Male, but they are generally neutral about gender in general.

Anatomy

Their teeth are sharp, their upper canines being unique looking, having the main part being a long sharp thick needle while the two shorter parts and still a bit long but not really needle-like, just sharp and pointy. so they can catch fish easier in their mouth. Their teeth can pierce flesh easily and rarely use it on when defending themselves, mostly preferring to be gentle and passive.

They are Omnivores but they do prefer to eat sea creatures that they can fit in their mouth, or eat anything that is dead already no matter the size. They do eat the sea flora. Their skin is very slippery to the touch, so it’s hard to hold them or grip onto them. The only parts that aren’t really like that are their doe clawed feet which are webbed, their horns. (Unless the horns are coral, then they might have slippery horns IF the coral is wet), or their tail which is hard like a prawns tail on the outside.

Their prawn tails have about 14 'segments' or whatever you call their big scaley parts on their tail usually.

Language

Kanpolo have been known to write on the walls of their homes, caves, wherever they happen to stay. A lot of the time telling stories, maybe a message for help or just a way to tell who was there. It’s sort of like marking territory for them sometimes too. But it’s usually to write their names alongside their relatives, sort of like a family tree in a way. It keeps track of the generations, you could say.

Kanpolo have also been known to share their language with the Woobar, who also speak and write in their language, though they may pronounce things differently than them. This is mainly due to generations of trading and communication between the species.

Liz'aes

Scientific Classification:

  • Kingdom: Animalia
  • Phylum: Chordata
  • Class: Amphibia
  • Order: Anthrosavras
  • Family: Schididae
  • Genus: Schidia
  • Species: Liz'ae
  • Binomial Name: Schidia exypanthropoi
The Liz’ae are a lizard-like humanoid species. Most of them live on a completely artificial planet, since their homeworld was destroyed, but some small colonies are still scattered across the galaxy. Liz’ae look like humans, but with scales scattered across their bodies, lizard tails, and antennae that assist their sense of smell. They are most often calm and non-confrontational by nature, with a big cultural emphasis on family and community, although there are exceptions to every rule. They are very accepting of new creatures and visitors.

Governmemt & Economy

The Liz’ae’s current government is a Republic, meaning that no one is above the law. Absolute power is not held by any one person or group. The federal government is very small, for the most part just handling the national military and foreign affairs. In order for the president to make a law or policy that extends over all the sectors, the vast majority of the senators have to vote in favor of it.

The Liz’ae are given great freedom to do whatever they want with their money inside the law and taxes are very low. This being said, welfare programs and free healthcare are either very small or nonexistent in most sectors, so taking care of poor Liz’ae is mostly funded by charities that the people put together instead of the government. It has proved highly effective, as the Liz’ae are a fairly generous people.

At the head of the federal government is the president. His powers are limited by the senate. Elections for that office are held once every five years. The Liz’ae are very prosperous and their planet is generally regarded as a good place to invest. They import goods just as fast as they export goods. Their government generally doesn’t give loans to other planets that are in trouble. Instead, they encourage the citizens to donate to relief projects that they send. Federal money is rarely used unless the need is very urgent.

History

  • (542 - 300ish BD - Before Destruction) • Their world lived in peace for the first three hundred years before being discovered by an outside species.
  • (300ish - 1 BD) • After that, they did their best to keep up with the rest of the galaxy and defend themselves.
  • (1 - 34 AD - After Destruction) • But they were eventually overcome by another species and their homeworld was destroyed. A small percentage who were either already offworld or were saved by a different alien species were taken elsewhere and hidden. They lived without a homeworld for several decades.
  • (34 - 54 AD) • A completely artificial planet is built over the course of twenty years as the Liz’ae thrive on other planets but want a place to form a central government.
  • (54 - 56 AD) • A small amount of Liz’ae move to the new planet in order to make it safe and liveable. Most do not move, as they have already established themselves on other planets and are thriving there.
  • (56 AD) • They make a central government and grant all Liz’ae automatic citizenship in order to encourage more of their kind to move there. A mass migration occurs as close to 90% of all the other Liz’ae make the decision to establish themselves there.
  • (56 AD) • There is a housing and job crisis with the mass migration, which eventually burns itself out.
  • (57 - 60 AD) • The Liz’ae firmly establish themselves on their new planet and begin importing and exporting goods. Their economy thrives.
  • (65 AD) Slavery is abolished, along with drugs used for non-medical purposes. There is some protestation but is quickly tamped down. Black markets to distribute them start to spring up.
  • (78 AD) • The Liz’ae make their own military because of people attacking their trade ships. They also establish a more effective police force. Crime rates on planet and off planet go down.
  • (82 AD) • the planet becomes fully occupied, making the population density go up in the less inhabited areas. Privately owned gardens and livestock get more valuable.
  • (107 AD) • Non-Liz’ae off worlders start going to their planet more and more. Immigration plans are put into place to give non-Liz’ae’s citizenship.
  • (125 AD) • The Liz’ae successfully repel an invasion in a single day. The government declares it a national holiday.
  • (Present Day) • The Liz’ae have gradually become more and more interested in offworld careers, and their planet is a thriving metropolis with a very good economy.

Main World

The Liz’ae’s new world is almost completely city. The livestock and crops are highly valuable and grown in artificial environments that allow them to be grown as naturally as possible. Of course, the quality will never be as good as it once was since the original planet got destroyed. There are fifteen “sectors” that the Liz’ae live in. Each has its own governor and policing system.

The capital is called Velfar and sometimes the planet is called that as well, although people call it both Velfar and just Liz’ae. Each sector has its own capital as well, and tend to compete with each other a little bit for tourism and things like that. They also each have slightly different laws, as each sector is given a lot of individual freedom.

One landmark is the tallest building on the planet, often called the Melar Tower after the Liz’ae who designed it. Another is the largest artificial garden on the planet. It features ponds, a small forest, and lots of flora and fauna that grow wild. It requires a lot of care and a large staff but still remains one of the most popular places to visit for both onworlders and offworlders. The only downside is that it also allows parasites to grow with the rest of the things there, and Liz’ae who visit are heavily advised to schedule a checkup with a doctor afterwards.

Most diseases that plague the Liz’ae are ones that affect their gills, scales/skin, mouth, stomach, ears, and eyes. They can be especially prone to disease when they are stressed, and some diseases are only able to take hold when the host is stressed. Otherwise, they can lay dormant in the liz’ae’s body and infect others. For this reason, liz’aes have regular checkups to insure that they are not unknowingly spreading a disease or parasite.

Family & Reproduction

Liz’ae have a very family-centered community and are very focused on making connections with others. They are very social and connected with their neighbors. A family typically consists of two parents and more than one child. Large families are very common, and it is not uncommon for Liz’ae children to have around three or four siblings. Children being adopted out is very very rare, so most stay with their biological parents.

Divorce is very uncommon in the Liz’ae community, with two Liz’ae often being together until one of them dies. If one dies and the other is still young, it is common for them to get married again after the grief lessens. Female Liz’ae keep a fertilized egg inside them for up to two weeks, then lay them in a pool of water so that they are completely submerged. The egg is normally fairly small, unless it is a twin egg. Eggs with twins or more are very very rare. The eggs take about two months to hatch. If they hatch earlier because something is wrong with the egg, it can be a problem for the child, but this is rare.

Education

All Liz’ae are homeschooled through ten grades (plus preschool and kindergarten), starting preschool at age three and ending homeschool at age sixteen. The program is government-approved and teaches both the “hard sciences” as well as the fine arts. Every Liz’ae (unless excused by some mental or physical disability,) takes at least one sport for the purpose of doing team-building exercises and physical development, but this is just a tradition, not required by law. Most Liz’ae also choose to learn an instrument, sometimes more than one. This includes singing and choir. This is also a tradition not required by law.

After finishing grade ten, a Liz’ae can choose to specialize in a particular field by either going to a college or finding a mentor. Mentors are widely popular but not all Liz’ae can find or afford one. Colleges are usually the more economic option. Mentors are usually very old and well-established in their field. To become a mentor, one has to take an incredibly difficult test that has both questions to be answered and hands-on work. Most Liz’ae are not able to pass the tests but are still able to teach at a college. The Liz’ae education system has proved to be sufficiently effective.

Holidays & Calendar

The Liz’ae calendar has 365 days and 12 months (with one leap day), like our calendar. For simplicity’s sake, we are going to label the months as they appear on our calendar instead of giving them new names.

Holidays Connected to Ours

  • Christmas for the Liz’ae is to celebrate a spirit of fellowship with others, and the connection between friend.
  • Thanksgiving for the Liz’ae is to celebrate contentment and being thankful for all that they have, like our Thanksgiving. They simply call it the Day of Thanks.
  • New Years could be described as being celebrated almost religiously across the sectors.
  • Valentine's Day is called the Festival of Youth, but is celebrated the same way.
  • Instead of Easter, they Celebrate the Springtime Festival. This is basically a month-long celebration of the turning of the seasons, even though their new planet does not have seasons any longer.
  • Mother’s Day and Father’s Day are also both celebrated on their usual dates and are called the same. Because Liz’ae normally have strong family ties, they are larger events than on earth.
  • Leap day is celebrated like April Fools’ day here. They do not have an April Fools’ day on April 1st.
  • Other holidays, (such as St. Patrick’s day,) are not usually celebrated by many Liz’ae so they will not be listed here, but may have their own event.

The Sectors

The different sectors on their planet do not have individual names, and are simply called “sector one,” “sector two,” etc. Each sector has its own “prime city,” which basically means that it's the capital of the sector. They are the largest cities in their sectors and also usually have the most offworld visitors. Velfar, the capital of their nation, is where the federal government is situated and has little to do with the individual sector’s governments. Each sector has its own specialty, and sectors can sometimes be competitive when it comes to making money and attracting tourists.

Sector 1

Has vast underground farms that specialize in growing crops. Most are aquatic plants. Its prime city is named Chaaldon.

Sector 2

Specializes in importing and exporting goods, although it typically buys them from other sectors. Its prime city is named Plego.

Sector 3

Has more colleges devoted to art than any other sector, and is their primary craft. Its prime city is named Antaville.

Sector 4

Specializes in turning out scientists. Its prime city is named Entohull.

Sector 5

Has more farms devoted to livestock than any of the other sectors. They also have the best quality. Its prime city is named Prin.

Sector 6

Specializes in military technology and weapons. It is much less popular with offworlders as it is highly secretive for obvious reasons. Its prime city is named Eryton.

Sector 7

Specializes in training Liz’ae to either be a part of the military or the police force. Most Liz’ae who want to be a part of the police force in their sector go to Sector 7 for training instead of training in their home sector. It is also not popular with offworlders. Its prime city is named Vonsall.

Sector 8

Doesn’t have great exports like most of the sectors, but it is by far one of the most popular with offworlders as it has the Ashtali garden. Its prime city is named Utoria.

Sector 9

Is the only sector that can compare with sector 8 for tourism as it has the capital city, Velfar. Otherwise, it is somewhat unremarkable. Its capital city is named Braudale.

Sector 10

Specializes in turning out mechanics and architects. Its prime city is named Breka.

Sector 11

Specializes in building spaceships, mostly for travel. Its prime city is named Onabury.

Sector 12

Specializes in the culinary arts. Its prime city is named Zreigh.

Sector 13

Specializes in fake gems, as producing them is very cheap and the quality remains good. Some more disreputable Liz’ae even take them to other planets privately and pass them off as the real things. Its prime city is named Cusrith.

Sector 14

Specializes in medicine and pharmaceuticals. Its prime city is named Akagas.

Sector 15

Specializes in clothing and the fashion industry. It also has the tallest building on the planet. Its prime city is named Andstin.

Young Heavians take about a year before they can start flying, so they often cling to their parents feathers and hitch a ride while maturing. Heavians usually leave their haven around 3 years old, but some do choose to stay. Safer in a haven, but may be forced to leave if the haven is too large. Heavians have also been known to prey on Kanpolo children, especially in the winter when they’re hungry. If they’re solitary though and it’s summer, the child will probably just confuse it and approaching with caution. Investigate it and then fly off. If it isn’t hungry, that is. They don’t kill what they can’t eat.

Parenting

Heavians are liked birds! They would create nests in mountain caves and would let them incubate in the cold, once their chick(s) have hatched. One parent would go out to hunt whilst the other stays with its young and protects them. Domesticated Heavians see Kanpolo children as their own, often bringing them offerings of their side. Either pretty things or just food.

Trivia

  • Before Heavians were declared a species, Kanpolos that spotted them would wish upon them, like shooting stars.
  • Summer is the optimal time to befriend a Heavian, they tend to graze on food or pick up smaller prey.
  • Heavians are very vocal! You may have heard a Heavian cry before, even if distant. They love to try and “talk” with their owner too.
  • Haven leaders usually have the longest tail ribbons/feathers or the largest wingspan.
  • A group of Heavians are called Havens, they’re normally in havens of 2-5 but it isn’t uncommon to find them solitary.
  • Elusive, usually soaring at altitudes that normally can’t be reached.
  • They nest in mountain caves, always having two parents present, (like birds; one parent hunts and the other watches over their young. Protecting them).
  • Heavians are usually only domesticated by nobles or the rich.
  • They’re rumoured to have a low population, due to mostly being hidden so that estimate tends to be lower than it actually is.
  • There is a Heavian Breeder known so far in Jovalan, but nobody knows the precise location and are difficult to get ahold of. Best bet is having a noble/rich folk friend to tell you where they may be. Most Kanpolos riding a Heavian probably received it from this breeder. They don’t give out two Heavians of opposing genders, because breeding them makes good money and they don’t want competition.

Noodle Moths

Scientific Classification:

  • Kingdom: Animalia
  • Phylum: Chordata
  • Subphyla: Vertebrata
  • Class: Mammalia
  • Order: Dracona
  • Family: Ophiskórodae
  • Genus: Ophiskóros
  • Species: Noodle Dragon Moth
  • Binomial Name: Ophiskórodae Makrýs
Noodle Moths, otherwise known as “Noodle Dragon Moths” are a open species! Which are like those long dragons, but really fluffy and moth-like! Noodle Dragon Moths are primarily found in the Arctic, because of their thick fluffy coats they can handle very cold conditions. But depending on the Noodle Moth, some may not be found in the Arctic. It really depends on their diets, for example; Nalangil is considered Omnivorous, but it prefers to eat flowers. Like a butterfly it uses its long weird sticky tongue to slurp up the pollen or sometimes it straight up eats the whole flower.

Noodle Moths range from giant to small as a Earth Moth. Noodle Dragon Moths are often called something specific depending on their size, for the smallest size (Earth moth size), they’re called Mini Noodles, Noodle Moths that are about thigh or knee height to a Kanpolo are often called Average Noodles and the big buggers, (Giant size) are often called Giant Noodles. Giant Noodles are big enough to be ridden by a Kanpolo, even a Deep Sea Kanpolo could ride one they’re that big! They’re probably as big as the Cedave or possibly bigger. Average and Mini Noodles are often kept as pets rather than mounts like the Giant Noodles.

Lifestyle & Behavior

These noodles are what you could consider the “Fairies of Jovalan”, often described as beautiful, elegant and friendly looking, some seem to possess powers, though, those noodles are quite few in number. The most common of the noodles are the minis and are more often found outside the Arctic. The little noodles when found, are often very friendly and can sometimes be seen curiously watching and studying creatures. Especially the Kanpolo, some Kanpolo have recounted that the little critters would sit on their fingers and sit there with their wings opening and closing, as if it was like a little butterfly. Perhaps maybe lick and nibble on their head fins which would tickle and make them giggle. While others have said they felt like the little critters were speaking to them.

Some recall as cubs that the mini noodles would mischievously play with them and sometimes were led away from their families. Becoming lost, but more often than not would be found with a group of more noodles. The little critters seem to really like children as they were more playful than the adults. There have been a few stories of average or giant noodles attacking Kanpolo or other creatures, though, these aggressive behaviours are quite rare. Most likely they’re from carnivorous noodles.

Woobars

Scientific Classification:

  • Kingdom: Animalia
  • Phylum: Chordata
  • Class: Chondrichthyes
  • Subclass: Elasmobranchii
  • Order: Carcharhiniformes
  • Superorder: Batoidea
  • Family: Diavolópsaridae
  • Genus: Diavolópsaro
  • Species: Woobar
  • Binomial Name: Diavolópsaro Anthrosári
Woobar are a primarily aquatic, semi-aquatic ray-like species with the face of a flying gurnard, with tentacle arms and 2 pairs of stub legs. They have a fish-like tail and a sail on their back with Rhinopores on their face! They’re smooth and slick like a sea slug or ray when you touch them.

Lifestyle & Behavior

Woobar live a majority of their life in the ocean, commonly found around volcanoes or warmer parts of the ocean. They cannot be out of the water for too long as their body isn’t really made for oxygen. So if they’re living on land they will stay close to water sources to not suffocate and dry out. Woobar are often found alone, as they’re not as social as a species like the Kanpolo and are considered solitary. But sometimes they are found in a group of 5 or 3 if they’re together. Usually they’re a family of Woobar and they aren’t very fond of sharing their territory with other Woobar.
